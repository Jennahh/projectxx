One in Seven California Students Must Get
a COVID-19 Vaccine Before the State Deadline

By Jennah Haque, Melissa Newcomb, and Caroline Ghisolfi

More than 40 California districts are enforcing COVID-19 vaccine requirements ahead of next year’s
state mandate. And some are stricter than Gov. Gavin Newsom’s plans, according to an
investigation by the Peninsula Press.

No California agency, including the California Department of Education, is keeping track of
these policies. The Peninsula Press contacted all 942 public school districts to create the first
living database recording the state patchwork of COVID-19 requirements.

Information collected from over 650 school districts reveals that over one million students are
affected by a wide range of vaccine policies. Some apply only to new employees, athletes or
children attending overnight school trips. Others affect the entire school population.

Just under 300 districts couldn’t be reached for comment after several attempts to contact
district superintendents, board members and administrative staff via email and phone. Ten
districts refused to release information about their COVID-19 vaccine requirements.

San Diego Unified, Los Angeles Unified and five other districts with over 20,000 students
approved mandates for students, staff or both. Like these districts, over 80 percent of those
enforcing independent policies lean Democratic. Only 7 districts with independent mandates
lean Republican, according to data provided by the California Secretary of State.
